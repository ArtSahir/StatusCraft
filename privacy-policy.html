01 Introduction

StatusCraft (the “app”) is published by ArtSahir. This policy describes the personal information we handle when you use the app on Android, the choices you have, and the third-party services that power features such as cloud sync, advertising, and crash reporting. By using StatusCraft you agree to the practices described here.

Last updated: May 5, 2026.

02 What we collect

StatusCraft works without an account, so we do not ask for your name, phone number, address book, or location. The data the app does see falls into three buckets:

On-device data (never leaves your phone)

  • Your favorites, diary notes, daily schedules, and theme/notification preferences are stored locally using Android's DataStore and Room database.
  • The list of dismissed in-app notifications and recent search categories.

Aggregate, non-identifying data sent to our backend

  • Anonymous share/copy counts per phrase so we can show a global “Most shared” section. We use Firebase Firestore for this. No user id is attached.

Diagnostic & analytics data (only after you opt in)

  • Crash logs via Firebase Crashlytics and aggregate event counters via Google Analytics for Firebase. Both stay disabled until you flip the “Usage analytics” toggle in Settings.

03 How we use it

We use the data above strictly to operate, secure, and improve StatusCraft — never to build advertising profiles or sell information. Specifically:

  • Display the content you requested (greetings, quotes, notes).
  • Remember the language, theme, and feature preferences you set.
  • Surface community trends like the “Most shared” rail.
  • Diagnose crashes so we can ship fixes (only with your consent).
  • Show ads to keep the app free (see section 05).

05 Ads & consent

StatusCraft shows ads through Google AdMob. AdMob may collect a limited set of identifiers (such as the Android Advertising ID, IP address, and ad-interaction events) to show ads and measure performance. If you live in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the app uses the Google UMP consent form the first time you launch it. You can revisit your choice at any time from Settings → Manage ad preferences when applicable.

Android 13 (API 33) and above gives you a system-level “Delete advertising ID” switch in Settings → Privacy → Ads. We honour that signal automatically.

06 Storage & retention

Local data lives only on your device and is wiped when you uninstall the app or clear its storage. Anonymous share counts written to Firestore are retained for as long as the feature is operational. Crash and analytics events follow Firebase's default retention windows, which you can read about in Firebase's privacy and security center.

07 Children

StatusCraft is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has used the app and provided data to a third-party service such as AdMob, please email us and we will help take appropriate action.

08 Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under the GDPR, the CCPA, or similar local laws — to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict the data we hold about you, and to withdraw any consent you previously gave. Because StatusCraft does not store personally-identifying records on the server side, the most direct way to exercise these rights is to:

  • Toggle off “Usage analytics” in Settings to stop Crashlytics & Analytics collection going forward.
  • Use Android's “Delete advertising ID” control to disable personalized ads.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all local data.
  • Email us at the address below for any further request.

09 Security

Traffic between the app and Firebase travels over TLS 1.2+. We use Firebase App Check with the Play Integrity API to verify that requests come from a genuine, unmodified StatusCraft install. While we apply industry-standard safeguards, no system is perfectly secure; please do not store sensitive secrets in your notes.

10 Changes to this policy

When we change anything material we update the “Last updated” date at the top and publish the new version at the same URL. Continued use of StatusCraft after a revision means you accept the revised policy.
